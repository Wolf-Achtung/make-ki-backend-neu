4 Pillars of AI Readiness

Executive takeaway: Start where you are, prioritise quick wins and build a pragmatic, auditable operating model.

1) Governance & Compliance

Goal: lawful, transparent and auditable AI use. Policies, roles, registers and DPIA are non‑negotiable.

  • Company‑wide AI policy incl. role definitions and Responsible‑AI principles.
  • AI inventory/registry of all use cases and data flows; versioned approvals.
  • Risk classification (EU AI Act), DPIA/DSFA and release gates.
  • Templates: DPIA, processor agreements, TOMs; evidence & logs.

2) Security & Risk Management

Minimise data leakage, model misuse and supply‑chain risks; build resilience.

  • Threat modelling for each use case; abuse/jailbreak testing.
  • DLP controls, logging, vendor evaluation (residency, export, SLAs).
  • Incident response runbooks and audit routines.
  • Role‑based enablement and do/don’t patterns.

3) Value & Processes (Use Cases & Operating Model)

Prioritise measurable value with a lean operating model.

  • Use‑case backlog with ROI/TCO; impact vs. effort; risk vs. maturity.
  • Pipeline: discovery → pilot → rollout → operations.
  • Quality gates: prompts, tests/evals, Human‑in‑the‑Loop.
  • Toolchain integration and access model.

4) Enablement & Culture

Teams that use AI safely, confidently and creatively.

  • Role profiles (e.g. Prompt Specialist, AI Owner, Data Steward).
  • Training paths and micro‑learnings; communication kit & success stories.
  • Change plan with milestones and KPIs.
