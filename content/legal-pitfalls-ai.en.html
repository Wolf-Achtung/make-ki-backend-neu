Legal Pitfalls When Using AI in Companies

AI promises efficiency and speed — without a plan you risk friction, legal exposure and rework. These ten pitfalls and countermeasures are aligned with the EU AI Act timeline.

  1. Ignoring regulation too long. Plan for EU AI Act obligations early; retrofitting is costly.
  2. No inventory. Keep a mandatory AI registry with roles, risk class, duties and evidence.
  3. No lifecycle guardrails. Standardise selection, testing, approvals, documentation and monitoring.
  4. Trusting laws, forgetting contracts. Put information duties, security, audit, changes and IP into contracts.
  5. Overlooking IP. Clarify chain of rights for models, training/input data and outputs; do input/output checks.
  6. Under/overestimating AI literacy. Provide role‑based training and refreshers.
  7. No change control/agents. Define re‑classification triggers, enforce HiTL and kill‑switch.
  8. AI Act tunnel vision. Consider product law, CRA, DSA, Data Act and sector rules.
  9. Ignoring tech change. Establish a tech radar and periodic reviews (performance/drift/bias).
  10. Starting without a plan. Put the topic on the agenda before first use cases go live.
